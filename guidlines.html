    • General Guidelines & Prerequisites

    Determination of cleanliness conditions


    The receiving vessel's holds, trucks, wagons and other storage facilities should be thoroughly inspected for cleanliness to protect the cargo from potential pollution and contamination.

    Loading / Discharging Operations


    Proper handling of cargo is essential at every stage of the supply chain. Goods must be inspected at each stage.

    To accurately determine the weight it is necessary to ensure that proper equipment verification and weighing controls are in place. This task must be performed by well-qualified independent surveyors.

    Quality is determined based on a sample. To ensure accuracy, sampling requires the expertise of independent surveyors with professional skill and extensive experience in each product, minimizing risks associated with the condition of goods at the time of sampling. This should be handled by qualified inspectors and recognized laboratories.

    Certification and Reporting


    Certificates must be issued by an independent surveyor recognized by international trade entities, particularly banks and federations mentioned in contracts. Reports are generated upon task completion, containing information to identify the inspected lot and a summary of actions taken.
